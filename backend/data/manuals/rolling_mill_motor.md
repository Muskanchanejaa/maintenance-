title: Rolling Mill Drive Motor Manual
section: Thermal and vibration limits
---
If winding temperature exceeds the thermal band while vibration rises above the operating band, reduce load and inspect drive-end and non-drive-end bearings. High torque demand, speed sag, and heat-dissipation failure indicators point to overload, rotor drag, lubrication breakdown, or coupling misalignment. Do not continue campaign operation until lubrication and alignment are verified.

title: SOP RM-17 Motor Hot Bearing Response
section: Immediate response
---
For hot bearing suspicion, isolate in the next safe pass gap, notify rolling supervisor, record thermography image, check grease line, inspect coupling insert, and perform laser alignment before restart. If vibration remains high after lubrication, move to shutdown repair.

title: Failure Analysis: Stand 2 Motor Bearing
section: Findings
---
The April event showed varnished grease, pitted bearing race, and coupling elastomer cracks. The recommended prevention was shorter grease inspection interval during high-load rolling campaigns and coupling inventory reservation before summer operation.
